About Cruciform Church

Our Story & Mission

Founded on the conviction that the preached Word of God is the primary means of grace, Cruciform Church has been discipling believers since 2008.

Our Story

Cruciform Church began in 2008 when a small group of believers gathered in a living room with a simple conviction: that the Bible, faithfully preached and carefully applied, is sufficient for all of life. What started as twenty people has grown, by God's grace, into a congregation of over 600 members and regular attenders.

The name "Cruciform" reflects our theology and our ambition: we want every aspect of church life to be shaped by the cross. We are convinced that the Gospel of Jesus Christ — His perfect life, atoning death, and bodily resurrection — is not just the entry point of the Christian life but its ongoing power and pattern.

Our Mission

"To glorify God by making and maturing disciples of Jesus Christ through the ministry of His Word."

We pursue this mission through expository preaching, biblical counseling, Gospel-centered small groups, and active engagement with both our local community and the global Church through our mission partners.

What We Believe

Statement of Faith

Cruciform Church holds to the historic Reformed faith as summarized in the Westminster Confession of Faith and the 1689 London Baptist Confession, with the Holy Scriptures as our supreme standard.

I.

The Holy Scriptures

−

We believe the sixty-six books of the Old and New Testaments are the inspired, inerrant, and infallible Word of God — the complete, authoritative, and sufficient rule for all matters of faith, life, and practice. God the Holy Spirit moved human authors so that their words are truly the words of God (2 Tim. 3:16–17; 2 Pet. 1:20–21).

Key References: 2 Timothy 3:16–17 · 2 Peter 1:20–21 · Psalm 19:7–11 · John 17:17
II.

The Triune God

+

We believe in one God, eternally existing in three co-equal Persons — Father, Son, and Holy Spirit — each fully and completely God, yet one in essence, glory, and nature. The Triune God is the creator, sustainer, and sovereign Lord of all creation.

Key References: Deuteronomy 6:4 · Matthew 28:19 · 2 Corinthians 13:14 · John 1:1–3
III.

Jesus Christ — His Person & Work

+

We believe that Jesus Christ is the eternal Son of God, who became man (without ceasing to be God) by being conceived of the Holy Spirit and born of the virgin Mary. He lived a perfectly righteous life, died as a substitutionary atonement for the sins of His people, rose bodily from the dead on the third day, ascended to the right hand of the Father, and will return visibly and bodily to judge the living and the dead.

Key References: John 1:1, 14 · Isaiah 53 · Romans 3:21–26 · 1 Corinthians 15:1–8
IV.

The Holy Spirit

+

We believe the Holy Spirit is the third Person of the Trinity, co-equal and co-eternal with the Father and the Son. He convicts the world of sin, righteousness, and judgment; regenerates, indwells, baptizes, seals, and sanctifies every true believer; and produces the fruit of godliness in their lives.

Key References: John 16:8–11 · Romans 8:9–17 · Galatians 5:22–23 · Ephesians 1:13–14
V.

Salvation by Grace Through Faith

+

We believe that salvation is entirely by God's sovereign grace, through faith alone, in Christ alone, to the glory of God alone. Salvation includes regeneration, justification, adoption, sanctification, and glorification — all secured by Christ's obedient life and atoning death, applied by the Holy Spirit, and received through repentance and faith.

Key References: Ephesians 2:8–9 · Romans 3:21–31 · John 3:3–8 · Titus 3:5–7
VI.

The Church

+

We believe the universal Church comprises all true believers in every age and nation. The local church is an assembly of baptized believers, voluntarily gathered under the authority of Christ and His Word, committed to the preaching of the Gospel, the right administration of baptism and the Lord's Supper, and the exercise of biblical church discipline.

Key References: Matthew 16:18 · Ephesians 4:11–16 · Acts 2:41–47 · Hebrews 10:24–25
VII.

Last Things

+

We believe in the personal, visible, bodily return of Jesus Christ, the bodily resurrection of all the dead, the final judgment of all people, and the eternal blessedness of the redeemed and eternal punishment of the wicked. We await a new heaven and new earth where righteousness dwells.

Key References: Acts 1:11 · 1 Thessalonians 4:13–18 · Revelation 20–22 · John 5:28–29
